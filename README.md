# BrightTV
README: Audience Viewership Dataset Analysis1. OverviewThis dataset captures detailed television viewership records, providing a granular look at "who" is watching, "what" they are watching, and "when" engagement is at its peak. The data is primarily focused on the South African market and includes demographic, geographic, and temporal dimensions.2. Data DictionaryColumnDescriptionuseridUnique identifier for the individual viewer.Name / SurnamePersonal identification of the viewer.EmailContact information for the user.GenderGender identity (Male, Female, etc.).RaceDemographic classification.Age / Age_GroupNumerical age and categorical bracket (e.g., Young Adult, Adult, Middle Age).ProvinceSouth African province location (e.g., Gauteng, Free State, Mpumalanga).ChannelsThe specific broadcast channel or live event (e.g., SuperSport, CNN, Boomerang).DATE / Month / DayThe calendar date and day of the week the viewing occurred.TimeThe start time of the viewing session.DurationTotal time spent on the channel (HH:MM:SS format).3. Observations on Data QualityPlaceholders: The dataset contains some "No Date", "No Time", and "No Duration" entries. These are filtered out when calculating engagement metrics to ensure accuracy.Demographic Filling: Some entries for Race and Gender are marked as "None" or are missing; however, the userid remains consistent for tracking behavior across multiple sessions.5. Intended UseThis file serves as the foundation for the CEO Executive Dashboard, focusing on:Market Share: Identifying which provinces drive the most traffic.Programming Strategy: Determining which channels retain viewers for the longest duration.Ad Placement: Mapping peak viewing hours (15:00) and peak days (Saturday) for maximum ROI.
